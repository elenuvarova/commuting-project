# Research Findings — Commuting Experience

> Synthesized from a deep-research pass (5 angles, 25 sources fetched, 111 claims extracted,
> 24/25 verified via 3-vote adversarial checking). All sources peer-reviewed/primary unless noted.
> These ground the persona (Alex), insights, "How might we", and the prototype concept.

## Headline

Commuting reliably lowers mood and induces stress — but **subjective experience moderates this far
more than raw distance**. How a commuter *appraises* the journey, how much *control* they feel, and
how close others are *physically* matter more than objective length or overall crowding.

## Verified findings (with confidence)

1. **Commuting depresses mood & induces stress** (high). Mood is lower during the commute than other
   daily activities; stress comes from congestion, crowding, unpredictability. (Broader mental-health
   direction is "inconclusive" in the lead systematic review — frame as a direction, not a law.)
   — Chatterjee et al. 2020; Liu, Ettema & Helbich 2022.
2. **Subjective appraisal + perceived control beat objective length** (high). Negative cognitive
   appraisal outstrips other stressors; **control is the single most powerful predictor** of commute
   stress; and appraisal is **malleable/trainable**. — De Reuver & Biron 2024; Chatterjee et al. 2020.
3. **The commute's cost is paid via emotional exhaustion** (high). Mental effort on the commute harms
   arrival-state and work performance *fully mediated by exhaustion*; long commutes → lower job
   satisfaction, higher turnover. — De Reuver & Biron 2024; Jachimowicz et al. 2021.
4. **The commute is a home↔work role transition** (high). Deliberately using it for *role-clarifying
   prospection* (mentally rehearsing the upcoming work role) makes commuters far less negatively
   affected — turning a chore into a benefit. This is the evidence base for "decompression / headspace".
   — Jachimowicz et al. 2021 (Organization Science, 3 field studies incl. a 4-week intervention, n=1,736).
5. **Mode hierarchy** (high): walking/cycling > car > public transport on satisfaction. Satisfaction
   **falls with duration** and **rises with company**. — Chatterjee et al. 2020.
6. **Driving is most stressful, and stressors are MODE-SPECIFIC** (high). A stressor for one mode is
   not a stressor for another → build **mode-aware** UX, not one generic flow. — Legrain et al. 2015.
7. **Transit negativity → anger more than anxiety** (medium). Overcrowding/personal-space invasion,
   noise, temperature, odor, unpredictability/delays, hostile interactions drive *irritation/anger*.
   — Sarmiento-Suarez et al. 2026 (Lima); Loo & Tsoi 2024 (Hong Kong, n=5,908).
8. **Felt crowding = immediate personal-space proximity, NOT overall density** (high). An app can
   reduce *experienced* crowding (guide to emptier cars, support psychological withdrawal via audio)
   without solving system capacity. — Evans & Wener 2007 (incl. cortisol + performance measures).
9. **Bus/transit stress = stop environment + crowding + hostile attitudes + unreliable timing** (high)
   → riders feel exhausted, irritated, anxious. — Loo & Tsoi 2024.
10. **Active travel (cycling) measurably improves mental health** (high): 15.1% fewer
    antidepressant/anxiolytic prescriptions over 5 yrs vs non-cyclists. — Ma et al. 2024 (n=378,253, IV analysis).
11. **Objective duration & mode still matter** (high) — real levers even though subjective factors
    dominate; surface shorter/active alternatives. — Liu, Ettema & Helbich 2022.

**Refuted (0-3):** overall crowdedness and weather did NOT have a *structural* impact on experiential
wellbeing in the 2022 systematic review → macro environmental conditions are **weaker** levers than
personal-space proximity, perceived control, and cognitive appraisal.

## Critical caveat for the concept

**Reappraisal/reframing reliably reduces *subjective/felt* stress but NOT physiological markers.** The
emerging patterns the brief asked about — biometric/heart-rate feedback, music-synced-to-pace,
gamification, "urban survival kit"/audio centrality — were **not validated** by this evidence set
(flagged in openQuestions). Implication: a concept built on **heart-rate biometrics as the success
signal is on shaky ground** (the intervention moves felt stress, not HR). The course's Mario example
(heart-rate game) is a *teaching* example, not an evidence-backed mandate — we should steer toward the
**well-evidenced levers instead.**

## Evidence-backed design levers (ranked)

1. **Restore perceived control** — reduce uncertainty (when to leave, will I make it, how full is it,
   am I going to be late). Strongest single predictor.
2. **Guided cognitive reframing + role-transition** — help the commuter mentally shift home→work (and
   work→home), rehearse the upcoming role, decompress. Trainable, evidence-backed "headspace".
3. **Mode-aware experience** — tailor to car / train / cycling / walking; never one generic flow.
4. **Reduce *felt* crowding** — personal-space cues, psychological withdrawal (audio), emptier-car guidance.
5. **Nudge toward active/satisfying alternatives** and add a sense of company where it helps.

## Implication for Alex (hybrid, Antwerp↔Brussels)

Hybrid work *amplifies* levers #1 and #2: an irregular commute means **more uncertainty** (which days,
which train, is it even worth going in) and a sharper **home↔office role switch** than a daily
commuter has. The sweet spot for the concept is **control/uncertainty-reduction + guided
role-transition/decompression**, in a **train (public-transport)** context — not a heart-rate game.

## Supplementary pass — tailoring Alex (3 focused research agents)

### A. The Antwerp ↔ Brussels corridor (real-world facts)
- **~41 km; IC train ~39–44 min** via Berchem/Mechelen to Brussel-Noord/Centraal/Zuid (Omio 2025).
- **Dec 2024: NMBS cut the corridor from 4 → 3 trains/hour** (to fit the new Amsterdam–Brussels
  Eurocity Direct). Remaining trains so full that "people are left behind on platforms every day";
  advocacy group called it "one of Belgium's busiest" routes (Brussels Morning, 2024). **Crowding is now a daily reality.**
- **Punctuality ~89.7% in 2024** — but "on time" = **under 6 min late**, and the figure **excludes
  cancellations and skipped stops**. The ombudsperson found trains skipping stops **~5×/day** (900+ in
  six months), criticised as inflating the metric (newmobility.news, 2024–25). → the *official* number
  is not the *felt* reliability.
- **Car alt:** E19 ~1h+ (Brussels = 3rd most congested city, TomTom 2021); Brussels parking **€24–30/day**;
  LEZ day pass **€35**. Train beats car on time+cost — useful to reinforce on a bad-delay day.
- **Flex Season Ticket** — purpose-built for hybrid commuters going **2–3 days/week**: buy **6/10/80/120
  travel days** used flexibly. Employer contributes **70.48%** (statutory, since 1 Feb 2026) → employee
  pays ~29.5% (SNCB, 2025–26). **The right ticket for Alex is a Flex 10, not a monthly.**
- **Official SNCB/NMBS app** (1M+ monthly users): real-time tracking, disruption push, in-app Flex-day validation.

### B. Hybrid-worker commuting patterns
- **"Should I even go in today?" is the #1 hybrid pain:** 38% say knowing *when/why* to come in is their
  biggest challenge; only 28% of orgs have team agreements (Microsoft Work Trend Index, 2022).
- **Each office day is a fresh cost/benefit calc:** office day ≈ $61 vs WFH ≈ $19 (Owl Labs 2024);
  shorter commute → 28% more inclined to come in.
- **"Choose-your-own-day" hybrid breeds empty, lonely offices** — people commute then still Zoom; lost
  coordination (Drake Univ. longitudinal, via The Conversation 2025).
- **Losing the commute removes a psychological "liminal space" → burnout;** a self-made "fake commute"
  (e.g., a short walk) restores recovery/detachment (Psychology Today 2023, summarizing Org. Psych. Review).
- **The irregular rhythm itself is a stressor** ("paradox management fatigue" from constant mode-switching).
- **Office days valued for people** (meeting 55%, mentoring 51%); **WFH days for focus** (51%) and balance (51%).

### C. App landscape & the white space
- **Transit/nav** (Transit, Citymapper, Moovit, Google Maps): surface the uncertainty but offer **no coping
  / no reframing**; the only "psychological" feature anywhere is Transit's stop-approach vibration.
- **Audio** (Spotify "Daily Drive", BPM mixes): commuters are a "captive audience" (71% of podcast listening
  in commuting hours) but treated as **ad inventory**; cadence features target **running**, not the commute.
- **Mindfulness** (Headspace has a 5-min "Mindful Commute"; Calm): **reframe** the commute (gratitude/
  "adventure") but **ignore the actual trigger — delay/uncertainty** — and aren't trip-aware or about role-transition.
- **Productivity rituals** (Sunsama shutdown ritual; Cal Newport): exist only as **desk software / blog advice**,
  never in-transit and never a *start-of-day* ramp-up.
- **Gamified/sustainable** (Pave Commute, Miles): rewards/nudges with **zero wellbeing content**.

**White space (the gap our concept fills):** *No app links (a) reducing commute uncertainty / restoring
perceived control with (b) a guided home↔work mental transition.* Mindfulness reframes but ignores the
trigger; transit apps show the trigger but offer no coping. Role-transition is unserved in transit.
**Hybrid workers are doubly underserved** — their commute is irregular, so the transition cue is inconsistent.
